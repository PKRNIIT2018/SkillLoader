# 🇸🇰 Slovakia: Visas, Permits & Legal Formalities
*Official comprehensive guide for short-term visits and long-term relocation.*

> ⚠️ **Disclaimer**: Immigration regulations change frequently. Always verify requirements with official Slovak authorities before travel or application. This content is for informational purposes only and does not constitute legal advice.

---

## ✈️ Section 1: Visiting Slovakia (Short-Stay)
*Stays up to 90 days within any 180-day period.*

### /visiting/short-stay-visa/
For citizens of countries that do not have a visa-waiver agreement with the EU/Schengen Area.

* **Schengen Visa (Type C):** Allows travel across all 29 Schengen countries for tourism, business, or family visits.
* **Validity Rule:** Maximum 90 days in any rolling 180-day period. Use the official [Schengen Visa Calculator](https://ec.europa.eu/assets/home/visa-calculator/calculator.htm) to verify compliance.
* **Fee:** €90 (Adults), €45 (Children 6-12), Free (Under 6).
* **Processing Time:** Standard 15 calendar days; extended cases up to 45 days.
* **Application Location:** Slovak embassy/consulate in your country of legal residence or citizenship.
* **Requirements:**
  - Completed application form (available on [mzv.sk](https://www.mzv.sk))
  - Valid passport (issued within last 10 years, valid 3+ months beyond intended departure, 2 blank pages)
  - Recent ICAO-standard photo (3x3.5 cm, light background)
  - Travel medical insurance covering minimum €30,000 for emergencies/repatriation across all Schengen states
  - Proof of accommodation (hotel booking, invitation letter, or rental agreement)
  - Proof of sufficient financial means: **€56/day** standard, **€26/day** if accommodation pre-paid
  - Proof of travel purpose (invitation letter, conference registration, tour itinerary)
  - Return/onward travel ticket or reservation
* **Biometrics:** Fingerprints and photo required at application (exemptions: children under 12, physical impossibility, heads of state/government)
* **Visa Sticker:** Check all details immediately upon receipt; errors must be corrected before travel.

### /visiting/visa-exempt-countries/
Citizens of over 60 countries can enter visa-free for tourism, business meetings, or family visits.

* **Key Countries:** USA, UK, Canada, Australia, Japan, South Korea, Israel, New Zealand, Singapore, UAE, Brazil, Argentina, Chile, Mexico, Malaysia.
* **Full List Reference:** Check Annex I of EU Regulation 2018/1806 on the [Ministry of Foreign Affairs website](https://www.mzv.sk).
* **Important (Late 2026):** Travelers from visa-exempt countries will require **ETIAS** (European Travel Information and Authorisation System) authorization prior to travel. Approx. fee €7, valid 3 years or until passport expiry.
* **Biometric Passport Condition:** Mandatory for nationals of Albania, Georgia, Moldova, Montenegro, North Macedonia, Serbia, and Ukraine.
* **Entry Conditions at Border:**
  - Valid passport or travel document
  - Proof of sufficient funds for stay
  - Proof of accommodation and return/onward travel
  - No entry ban in Schengen Information System (SIS)
* **Work Restriction:** Visa-free entry does **not** permit employment. Any paid activity requires appropriate work authorization.

### /visiting/entry-requirements/
* **Reporting Obligation:** All third-country nationals must report their stay to the Foreign Police within **3 working days** of arrival. *Note:* Hotels, hostels, and registered accommodation providers automatically fulfill this obligation for guests.
* **If Staying in Private Accommodation:** The host or visitor must register at the nearest Foreign Police department or via the [online reservation system](https://priblizne.sk/rezervacny-system-cudzineckej-policie/).
* **Documents to Carry:** Always have on hand:
  - Valid passport/ID
  - Proof of health insurance valid in Slovakia
  - Proof of sufficient financial means
  - Return ticket or proof of onward travel
* **Border Controls:** Slovakia applies Schengen border code. Border guards may request documentation proving purpose and conditions of stay.
* **Brexit Note:** UK citizens are now third-country nationals. Visa-free short stays apply, but work/residence permits are required for employment or stays >90 days.

## 🏠 Section 2: Living Long-Term (>90 Days)
*For study, business, employment, or family relocation.*

### /living-long-term/national-visa-type-d/
A long-stay national visa serving as a bridge while awaiting residence permit processing, or for specific short-term purposes.

* **Eligibility Categories:**
  - Applicants submitting temporary residence permit applications *in Slovakia*
  - Language school students (age 15+, minimum 25 lessons/week, accredited institution)
  - Scholarship holders under Government Resolution No. 731/2021 (Erasmus+, CEEPUS, bilateral agreements)
  - Highly qualified professionals under Government Regulation No. 521/2021
  - Family members of asylum/subsidiary protection beneficiaries
* **Duration:** Up to 1 year (non-extendable; must apply for residence permit before expiry if staying longer)
* **Fee:** €90 (standard residence applicants), €50 (cases of Slovak Republic interest), €30 (language students)
* **Processing Time:** 30 calendar days from application submission
* **Application Requirements:**
✓ Completed national visa application form (Slovak/English) ✓ Valid passport (min. 90 days validity beyond intended departure) ✓ Two ICAO-standard photos (3x3.5 cm) ✓ Document confirming purpose of stay (acceptance letter, scholarship award, residence application receipt) ✓ Proof of health insurance valid in Slovakia (min. €30,000 coverage) ✓ Proof of sufficient financial means: €56/day standard or €26/day with pre-paid accommodation ✓ For residence applicants: complete temporary residence application documents ✓ Clean criminal record extract (not older than 90 days, apostilled/legalized + official Slovak translation)
* **Application Submission:** In person at Slovak embassy/consulate. Biometric data collected at submission.
* **Important:** Type D visa does not automatically grant right to work. Employment authorization depends on underlying purpose.

### /living-long-term/temporary-residence/
* **Purpose Categories & Validity:**
| Purpose | Max Initial Duration | Renewal Possible |
|---------|---------------------|-----------------|
| Employment | Up to 5 years | Yes, if employment continues |
| Study | Duration of program + 90 days | Yes, for continued studies |
| Research & Development | Up to 5 years | Yes, with continued hosting agreement |
| Business/Self-Employment | Up to 5 years | Yes, with active business |
| Family Reunification | Matches sponsor's permit | Yes, if sponsor's status continues |
| Special Activity | Up to 2 years | Limited extensions possible |

* **Decision Periods:**
- 30 days: Study, Research, Special Activity, Family Reunification
- 90 days: Employment, Business, Other purposes
- *Clock starts when application is complete and accepted*

* **Document Validity Rules:**
- Criminal record extracts, bank statements, and similar supporting documents must not be older than **90 days** at time of submission
- All foreign documents require: (1) Apostille or consular legalization, AND (2) Official translation into Slovak by a court-certified translator

* **Application Submission Options:**
1. **From Abroad:** Submit at Slovak embassy/consulate in country of citizenship or legal residence
2. **From Slovakia:** Submit at competent Foreign Police Department *only if* legally present (e.g., on Type D visa, visa-free entry for eligible nationals)

* **Biometrics & Card Collection:**
- Fingerprints and photo taken at application
- Residence card must be collected in person within 30 days of approval notification
- Card validity dates align with approval decision, not collection date

* **Reporting Change of Circumstances:** Notify Foreign Police within 10 working days of any change in: address, marital status, passport details, or purpose of stay.

### /living-long-term/family-reunification/
* **Eligible Family Members:** Spouse, minor children (including adopted), dependent adult children, dependent parents (if sponsor is minor or dependent).
* **Sponsor Requirements:**
- Holds valid temporary/permanent residence permit in Slovakia, OR is an EU citizen exercising free movement rights
- Has stable, regular, and sufficient financial resources (no reliance on social assistance)
- Has adequate accommodation meeting Slovak housing standards
* **Application Process:**
- Family member applies from abroad at Slovak embassy, OR from Slovakia if legally present
- Sponsor provides: residence permit copy, proof of income, accommodation confirmation, family relationship documents (marriage/birth certificates, legalized + translated)
* **Processing Time:** 30 days from complete application
* **Permit Duration:** Matches sponsor's current residence permit validity
* **Work Rights for Family Members:**
- **First 12 months:** Generally cannot work unless sponsor holds EU Blue Card (then immediate access)
- **After 12 months:** Full access to labour market without separate work permit
- **Exception:** Family members of Slovak citizens or permanent residents have immediate work rights
* **Important:** Divorce, separation, or sponsor's permit revocation may affect derivative residence status. Seek legal advice for complex cases.

### /living-long-term/permanent-residence/
* **Long-Term Residence (5-Year Status):**
- **Eligibility:** 5 years of continuous legal temporary residence in Slovakia
- **Continuity Rule:** Absences not exceeding 6 consecutive months or 10 months total within the 5-year period
- **Study Adjustment:** Periods of residence for study count as 0.5 years per actual year of stay
- **New Requirement (Effective July 2025):** Proof of Slovak language proficiency at **A2 level** (CEFR) via accredited examination
- **Additional Requirements:** Stable income, adequate accommodation, clean criminal record, health insurance

* **Permanent Residence (Indefinite Status):**
- **Eligibility:** After holding long-term residence for additional period, or 5+ years total legal residence under specific conditions
- **Integration Requirement:** Demonstration of integration into Slovak society (language, cultural knowledge)
- **Benefits:**
  - Indefinite validity (card renewed every 10 years for security)
  - Full labour market access without permits
  - Enhanced protection against expulsion
  - Pathway to citizenship application after additional residency period

* **Application Process:**
- Submit at competent Foreign Police Department in Slovakia
- Processing time: Up to 180 days for permanent residence decisions
- Fee: €250 for long-term residence; €350 for permanent residence

* **Loss of Status:** Absence from Slovakia/EU for >12 consecutive months may result in loss of long-term/permanent status.

## 💼 Section 3: Working in Slovakia
*Legal frameworks for foreign employees and self-employed persons.*

### /working-in-slovakia/eu-citizens/
* **Freedom of Movement:** EU/EEA/Swiss citizens enjoy equal treatment with Slovak nationals regarding employment.
* **No Work Permit Required:** Can accept employment, start business, or provide services under same conditions as Slovaks.
* **Registration Requirement:** If staying longer than 3 months, register residence at Foreign Police within **30 days** of arrival. Required documents: valid ID/passport, proof of accommodation, proof of comprehensive health insurance.
* **Family Members:** Non-EU family members of EU citizens derive rights under Directive 2004/38/EC. Apply for "Residence Card of a Family Member of an EU Citizen" – processing within 6 months, but right to reside exists from application submission.
* **Documentation Tip:** While not legally required for employment, many employers request confirmation of registration. Keep copies of all submission receipts.

### /working-in-slovakia/single-permit-process/
The most common route for Non-EU workers seeking employment. Combines residence permit + work authorization in one procedure.

**Step-by-Step Process:**
1. **Vacancy Reporting (Employer Action):**
 - Employer reports vacant position to Central Office of Labour, Social Affairs and Family via [ISTP portal](https://www.istp.sk)
 - Must be done **minimum 20 working days** before foreign candidate's application submission
 - Labour Office assesses if suitable EU/EEA candidate available ("labour market test")

2. **Confirmation Issuance:**
 - If no suitable EU candidate found, Labour Office issues *"Confirmation of Possibility to Fill Vacancy with Third-Country National"*
 - This confirmation is valid for 90 days and must accompany residence application

3. **Application Submission (Employee Action):**
 - Submit "Application for Temporary Residence for Purpose of Employment" (Single Permit) at:
   - Slovak embassy/consulate abroad, OR
   - Foreign Police Department in Slovakia (if legally present)
 - Required documents:
   ```
   ✓ Completed application form (Slovak language)
   ✓ Valid passport + 2 photos
   ✓ Employment contract or binding job offer (min. 1 year for Blue Card track)
   ✓ Confirmation of vacancy possibility from Labour Office
   ✓ Proof of accommodation in Slovakia (rental agreement, property deed, or host declaration)
   ✓ Proof of health insurance valid in Slovakia
   ✓ Clean criminal record extract (apostilled + officially translated, <90 days old)
   ✓ Medical certificate confirming no serious public health risk
   ✓ Proof of sufficient financial means (bank statements, employment contract salary)
   ✓ Payment confirmation of administrative fee
   ```

4. **Decision & Card Collection:**
 - Processing: Standard 90 days; accelerated 30 days for strategic investments, R&D centres, or Blue Card applications
 - Upon approval: Collect residence card in person at Foreign Police within 30 days
 - Card will state "Employment" as purpose and may include employer/position details

**Important Notes:**
- Single Permit is **employer-specific**. Changing employer requires new application.
- Permit validity tied to employment contract duration (max 5 years initial grant).
- Family members of Single Permit holders have restricted work rights for first 12 months (see Family Reunification section).

### /working-in-slovakia/work-permit-separate-residence/
Required in specific scenarios where Single Permit does not apply:

* **Applicable Cases:**
- Holders of temporary residence for **family reunification** (first 12 months of residence)
- Holders of long-term residence issued by **another EU Member State** (first 12 months working in Slovakia)
- **Seasonal employment** (max 90 days of work within any 12-month period)
- Seafarers employed on vessels flying Slovak flag
- Certain intra-company transferees under WTO/GATS commitments

* **Two-Step Process:**
1. **Work Permit Application:** Employer applies to Labour Office with job offer, labour market test proof, candidate qualifications
2. **Residence Permit Application:** Employee applies for temporary residence for "employment" purpose using approved work permit

* **Key Restrictions:**
- Work permits are **non-transferable**: tied to specific employer, position, and location
- Changing employer, role, or work location = new work permit application required
- Validity: Maximum 2 years (or 5 years under specific international treaties)
- Does not automatically grant right to bring family members; separate family reunification application needed

* **Processing Times:**
- Work Permit: Up to 30 days from complete application
- Residence Permit: Additional 90 days after work permit approval
- *Plan accordingly: total timeline may exceed 4 months*

### /working-in-slovakia/eu-blue-card/
For highly qualified professionals from third countries.

* **Eligibility Criteria:**
- **Education:** University degree of at least 3 years (or 5+ years professional experience for ICT specialists)
- **Employment Contract:** Minimum 1-year duration (or 6+ months for renewal applications)
- **Salary Threshold (2026):** Gross annual salary ≥ **1.5x average Slovak wage** (approx. €2,100+ gross/month in 2026); reduced to **1.2x** for recent graduates of Slovak/Czech universities in shortage occupations
- **Position Classification:** Must be classified as "highly qualified" per Slovak occupation register

* **Advantages Over Standard Single Permit:**
- Faster processing: 30-day decision period for applicant and family members
- Enhanced intra-EU mobility: After 18 months in Slovakia, may move to another EU state under simplified Blue Card transfer rules
- Family members: Immediate labour market access (no 12-month waiting period)
- Validity: 3-5 years based on contract duration
- Pathway to long-term residence: Time on Blue Card counts fully toward 5-year requirement

* **Application Documents:** Additional to standard Single Permit:
- Authenticated university diploma + official Slovak translation
- Detailed CV and professional references
- Employer statement confirming highly qualified nature of position
- For ICT specialists without degree: evidence of 5+ years relevant professional experience

* **Renewal & Status Change:**
- Apply for renewal before expiry; processing 30 days
- Loss of highly qualified employment: 3-month grace period to find new qualifying position or change status
- After 2 years on Blue Card: may apply to change to standard employment residence if salary threshold no longer met

### /working-in-slovakia/employer-obligations/
Critical compliance requirements for Slovak employers hiring third-country nationals:

**Pre-Employment Phase:**
- Report vacancy to Labour Office via [ISTP system](https://www.istp.sk) minimum **20 working days** before foreign hire application
- Obtain *Confirmation of Possibility to Fill Vacancy* (unless exemption applies)
- Verify candidate's valid residence document authorizing employment *before work commences*
- Provide written employment contract meeting Slovak Labour Code minimums (wages, hours, leave, notice periods)

**At Employment Start:**
- Register employee with Social Insurance Agency (**before first day of work**)
- Register with public health insurance company within **8 working days** of employment start
- Notify Labour Office of employment commencement within **7 working days** (submit copy of signed contract)
- Provide employee with copy of work permit/residence card authorization documentation

**During Employment:**
- Maintain updated copy of employee's valid residence card on file at workplace
- Comply with all Labour Code provisions: minimum wage (€915/month gross in 2026), maximum working hours (40/week), paid leave (min. 4 weeks), occupational safety standards
- Report any changes to employment terms (salary reduction, role change, location change) to Labour Office within 7 working days
- Support employee's residence permit renewal process with updated employment confirmation

**At Employment End:**
- Notify Labour Office within **7 working days** of termination (provide termination agreement or notice documentation)
- Notify Foreign Police within **3 working days** if employment ends before residence permit expiry
- Deregister employee from Social Insurance and health insurance within **8 working days** of termination
- Provide employee with required employment documentation (work certificate, final settlement)

**Penalties for Non-Compliance:**
- Fines up to €330,000 for illegal employment of third-country nationals
- Revocation of work permit authorizations
- Temporary or permanent ban on hiring foreign workers
- Potential criminal liability for repeated or intentional violations

### /working-in-slovakia/exemptions-special-cases/
Situations where work authorization is not required or follows simplified rules:

* **University Students with Study Residence:**
- May work up to **80 hours per month** (approx. 20 hours/week) without separate work permit
- Must maintain full-time student status and academic progress
- Employment must not interfere with study obligations

* **Graduates of Slovak Universities:**
- Upon completing accredited Slovak degree program, eligible for **9-month residence permit for job-seeking**
- During this period: may work without separate work permit in any position
- If employed in qualified position: may transition to Single Permit or Blue Card

* **Researchers under Hosting Agreements:**
- Scientists admitted under Directive (EU) 2016/801 with approved Hosting Agreement
- May conduct research, teach, and undertake related activities without separate work permit
- Family members have immediate labour market access

* **Intra-Company Transferees (ICT):**
- Managers, specialists, or trainee employees transferred within multinational company
- Covered by WTO/GATS commitments or EU ICT Directive implementation
- Simplified procedure with maximum stay of 3 years (managers/specialists) or 1 year (trainees)

* **Short-Term Professional Activities (<30 days/year):**
- Artists, athletes, journalists, lecturers on specific short assignments
- Must not establish ongoing employment relationship in Slovakia
- Documentation of assignment purpose and duration required at border/application

* **Seasonal Workers:**
- Employment in tourism, agriculture, forestry for max 90 days within any 12-month period
- Requires specific seasonal work permit + residence authorization
- Employer must demonstrate seasonal nature of work and inability to fill with domestic/EU workers

* **Volunteers & Accredited Journalists:**
- Volunteers under accredited programs: no work permit needed for unpaid activities
- Journalists accredited by Slovak Ministry of Culture: may work for foreign media without Slovak work permit

## 🔄 Section 4: Renewals, Changes & Compliance

### /compliance/residence-renewal/
* **When to Apply:** Submit renewal application **before current permit expires**. Early applications accepted up to 180 days prior to expiry.
* **Processing Times:**
- 30 days: Study, Research, Special Activity, Family Reunification renewals
- 90 days: Employment, Business, other purpose renewals
* **Required Documents for Renewal:**
- Valid passport
- Current residence card
- Updated proof of accommodation
- Updated proof of financial means (bank statements, employment contract, business records)
- Updated health insurance confirmation
- Purpose-specific documents:
  - Employment: updated contract, employer confirmation, Labour Office notification
  - Study: enrollment confirmation, academic progress report
  - Business: trade license validity, tax compliance certificate, business activity proof
- Clean criminal record extract (if requested by authorities)
* **Biometrics:** New photo and fingerprints may be required at renewal application
* **Gap Prevention:** If application submitted on time, legal stay continues during processing even if card expires. Obtain confirmation receipt from Foreign Police.

### /compliance/change-of-circumstances/
* **Mandatory Reporting (Within 10 Working Days):**
- Change of residential address in Slovakia
- Change of passport or travel document details
- Change of marital status or family composition
- Loss or theft of residence card
- Change in purpose of stay (e.g., student to employee)

* **Changing Employer or Position (Single Permit Holders):**
- New employer must complete vacancy reporting and labour market test
- Employee must submit application for "change of purpose/employer" at Foreign Police
- Cannot commence work with new employer until new authorization granted
- Processing time: up to 90 days; plan transition carefully

* **Loss of Employment:**
- Notify Foreign Police within 10 working days of job loss
- Single Permit may be revoked if employment ends before permit expiry
- Options:
  - Apply for change to job-seeking residence (if eligible)
  - Apply for new Single Permit with different employer
  - Depart Slovakia before legal stay expires

### /compliance/document-authentication/
* **Foreign Documents Requirements:**
1. **Legalization:**
   - Countries party to Hague Apostille Convention: obtain Apostille from issuing country authority
   - Non-Apostille countries: consular legalization via Slovak embassy/consulate in document-issuing country
2. **Official Translation:**
   - Must be performed by translator certified by Slovak Ministry of Justice
   - Translation must be attached to original legalized document
   - Some documents (e.g., passports) may not require translation if in Czech/Slovak

* **Common Documents Requiring Authentication:**
- Birth certificates, marriage certificates, divorce decrees
- University diplomas and academic transcripts
- Criminal record extracts / police clearance certificates
- Powers of attorney, corporate documents for business applicants

* **Validity Period:** Most authenticated documents (criminal records, bank statements, medical certificates) must be issued within **90 days** of residence application submission.
